Data Visualization Activity - Complete Summary

Dataset

Video Game Sales (VGChartz 2024)

  • 64,016 games
  • $6.6 Billion total sales
  • Years: 1980-2024

The Task

  • ✅ Analyze the dataset
  • ✅ Identify ONE major insight
  • ✅ Create visualizations (5 charts)
  • ✅ Write a short narrative (7 sentences)
  • ✅ Prepare a 2-minute story presentation

Guiding Questions

1. Who is the audience?

Game developers and investors looking to maximize profit

2. What decision should they make?

What type of game to make and where to sell it

3. What evidence supports your story?

  • Action/Shooter games dominate sales
  • PlayStation has 51% market share
  • North America buys 51% of games

The One Major Insight

“Action and Shooter games on PlayStation, sold in North America, generate the most sales.”

7-Sentence Narrative

I looked at a dataset of 64,000 video games to find out which types of games sell the most. Action and Shooter games make the most money—together they make up 32% of all sales. The best-selling games are mostly GTA and Call of Duty, which are all action or shooter games. PlayStation is the biggest gaming platform, with 51% of the market. Most game sales come from North America, which makes up 51% of buyers worldwide. Building a game series pays off—Call of Duty earned $303 million across multiple titles. So the winning formula is simple: make an action or shooter game, release it on PlayStation, and target North American players.

The 5 Charts

Chart 1: Genre Breakdown (Bar Chart)

  • Shows sales by game category
  • Sports ($1,188M), Action ($1,126M), Shooter ($996M) lead
  • Insight: Action + Shooter = 32% of market

Chart 2: Top 10 Games (Bar Chart)

  • Shows best-selling games
  • GTA V appears 3 times (PS3, PS4, X360)
  • Call of Duty dominates with 5 entries
  • Insight: Action/Shooter games win

Chart 3: Console Wars (Donut Chart)

  • Shows platform market share
  • PlayStation: 51% | Nintendo: 26% | Xbox: 21% | PC: 3%
  • Insight: PlayStation dominates

Chart 4: Regional Sales (Donut Chart)

  • Shows where games are sold
  • North America: 51% | Europe: 29% | Japan: 10% | Other: 10%
  • Insight: Target Western markets

Chart 5: Top Franchises (Bar Chart)

  • Shows franchise total sales
  • Call of Duty: $303M | FIFA: $212M | GTA: $155M
  • Insight: Build a franchise for recurring revenue
